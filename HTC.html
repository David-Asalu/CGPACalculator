How to calculate your GPA/ CGPA

Step-by-step instructions on how to calculate GPA and CGPA for college students in Canada, Nigeria and the U.S.A.

Please note that grading systems may vary between institutions and countries, so these instructions are general guidelines. Always check with your specific school or university for any variations.

What is a GPA?

Your GPA (Grade Point Average) is a number that represents all of the letter grades you earned in either high school or college. Condensing the grades on your transcript into one number gives colleges and businesses a quick overview of your academic success and helps you keep track of your educational goals.

Fortunately, our GPA calculator handles all the computations, but it's essential to understand that these calculations are not mystical. Continue reading to discover the method of GPA calculation and grasp the different types of GPA scales.

Step-by-step instructions for your calculating GPA/CGPA:

United States of America (USA):

The GPA system in the USA is typically based on a 4.0 scale, with A being 4.0 and F being 0.0.

  1. Assign a numerical value to each letter grade:
    • A = 4.0
    • B = 3.0
    • C = 2.0
    • D = 1.0
    • F = 0.0
  2. Determine the credit hours for each course: Check your course syllabus or contact your school to find the credit hours assigned to each subject.
  3. Calculate the grade points for each course:
    • Multiply the numerical value of the grade by the credit hours for that course. For example, if you got an A (4.0) in a 3-credit hour course, the grade points would be 4.0 * 3 = 12.
  4. Total grade points:
    • Add up all the grade points for all the courses you have taken.
  5. Total credit hours:
    • Add up all the credit hours for all the courses you have taken.
  6. Calculate GPA:
    • Divide the total grade points by the total credit hours to get your GPA.

Canada:

The GPA system in Canada is similar to the one used in the USA, typically based on a 4.0 scale.

Follow the same steps as mentioned above for the USA to calculate your GPA.

The table below shows an example of a student who scored four As and 2 Bs in a semester and how her grade would be calculated.

To find the semester GPA, add all of the total points (51) and divide by the number of credit hours (14).

51 ÷ 14 = 3.64

Nigeria

  1. Grading Scale:
    • A = 5.0 (Excellent)
    • B = 4.0 (Very Good)
    • C = 3.0 (Good)
    • D = 2.0 (Fair)
    • E = 1.0 (Pass)
    • F = 0.0 (Fail)
  2. Determine the credit units/hours for each course: Check your course syllabus or contact your school to find the credit units or credit hours assigned to each subject. These credit units represent the weight or value of each course.
  3. Calculate the Grade Points (GP) for each course:
    • For each course, multiply the credit units of the course by the grade point corresponding to the grade earned in that course. For example, if you received a grade of B (4.0) in a 3-credit hour course, the grade points would be 4.0 * 3 = 12.
  4. Calculate the Total Grade Points (TGP) and Total Credit Units (TCU):
    • Add up all the Grade Points earned for all courses to get the Total Grade Points (TGP). Similarly, add up all the credit units for all the courses to get the Total Credit Units (TCU).
  5. Calculate the GPA:
    • To calculate your GPA, divide the Total Grade Points (TGP) by the Total Credit Units (TCU). The formula is as follows: GPA = Total Grade Points (TGP) / Total Credit Units (TCU)
  6. Calculate the Cumulative GPA (CGPA):
    • The Cumulative GPA (CGPA) represents the GPA calculated for all courses taken throughout your academic career.
    • Follow the same steps 3 to 5 for each semester or academic year to calculate the GPA for that specific period.
    • Keep track of the Total Grade Points (TGP) and Total Credit Units (TCU) earned from all semesters or academic years combined.
    • Add up all the Total Grade Points (TGP) and Total Credit Units (TCU) for all semesters or academic years.
    • Divide the cumulative Total Grade Points (TGP) by the cumulative Total Credit Units (TCU) to get your CGPA.
